Preamble

Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria 1999

We the people of the Federal Republic of Nigeria

Having firmly and solemnly resolve, to live in unity and harmony as one indivisible and indissoluble sovereign nation under God, dedicated to the promotion of inter-African solidarity, world peace, international co-operation and understanding

And to provide for a Constitution for the purpose of promoting the good government and welfare of all persons in our country, on the principles of freedom, equality and justice, and for the purpose of consolidating the unity of our people

Do hereby make, enact and give to ourselves the following Constitution:—

I

Chapter I

General Provisions

§ 1 Supremacy of the Constitution

(1)

This Constitution is supreme and its provisions shall have binding force on the authorities and persons throughout the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

Plain English

This Constitution is the highest law, and all government bodies and individuals in Nigeria must obey its rules.

Verified by Admin Constitution Supreme Law Binding Force Authorities Persons Federal Republic of Nigeria
(2)

The Federal Republic of Nigeria shall not be governed, nor shall any persons or group of persons take control of the Government of Nigeria or any part thereof, except in accordance with the provisions of this Constitution.

Plain English

Nigeria cannot be governed, and no individual or group can take control of the government, unless they follow the rules set out in this Constitution.

Verified by Admin Governance Control of Government Constitutional Compliance Rule of Law Federal Republic of Nigeria
(3)

If any other law is inconsistent with the provisions of this Constitution, this Constitution shall prevail, and that other law shall, to the extent of the inconsistency, be void.

Plain English

If any other law contradicts this Constitution, the Constitution takes precedence, and the conflicting part of that other law is invalid.
